(1) What is the independent variable? What is the dependent variable?

The independant variables are the conditions of the test(whether or not the colour and word name were different or the same. Therefore, the independant variables are the Congruent and Inconcruent conditions.

The dependant variable is the reaction time between stimulus and response. (i.e The time it takes participants to complete the task)

(2) What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? Specify your null and alternative hypotheses, and clearly define any notation used. Justify your choices.

Hypothesis:

Null Hypothesis:

The stroop effect does not interfere with participant's reaction times.

Alternative Hypothesis:

Conditions under which participants are tested have significantly different reaction times

$$H_0: \mu_{c} - \mu_{i} = 0$$ $$H_1: \mu_{c} - \mu_{i} \neq 0$$

Where $\mu_{c}$ represents the mean times of Congruent responses and $\mu_{i}$ represents the mean times of Incongruent responses.

(2b) We will perform a paired t-test:

  • the same individuals were treated to the two different conditions presented in the study(whether or not the colour and word name were different or the same), and we need compare the differences of the participants response times in order to determine the significance of the stroop effect
  • We are testing the differences before and after some treatment on the same individuals.
  • The ability to compare the means of the dataset for the pre and post test validates the benefit of this test selection
    • The sample size is small (24<30)
  • The population variance is unknown
  • We are assuming that the data is normally distributed.

Expected outcomes of the test

The response times under Incongruent conditions will be longer tham the response times under Congruent conditions.Results from the statistical test will prove that we reject the null in favour of the alternative.

Conclusion

The p-value is 4.1030, which is below the standard thresholds of $\alpha$ = 0.05 or 0.01 as our level of significance and our t-statistic > t-critical (8.0207 > 1.711),so we reject the null hypothesis in favour of the alternative. Therefore, we can say there is a statistically significant difference in Congruent and Incongruent conditions. We conclude that the participants’ reaction time of the task under the incongruent condition is significantly longer than that of the congruent condition.
